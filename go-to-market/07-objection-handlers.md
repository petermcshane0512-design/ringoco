# OBJECTION HANDLERS — Top 8

**Rule:** Acknowledge → Reframe → Ask. Never argue. Never defend.

---

## 1. "AI won't sound natural / customers will hate it"

**Acknowledge:** "Yeah I get it — most AI receptionists from 2 years ago were brutal."
**Reframe:** "Honest answer: ours sounds like a real person 9 outta 10 times. The 1 outta 10 — caller knows but doesn't care because their AC is broken and they got booked in 90 sec."
**Ask:** "Pick up ur phone right now and call (765) 237-1335 — pretend ur a customer w a broken furnace. Tell me what u hear."

[Let them call. Wait. They'll come back impressed or with specific complaints. Either is useful.]

---

## 2. "$497 is too much"

**Acknowledge:** "Fair. Compared to Rosie at $149 it looks high."
**Reframe:** "Difference is what u get. Rosie answers calls. We answer calls + chase quotes + collect on past-due + manage reviews. The all-in office manager u'd hire is $5K/mo. We're 1/10th that."
**Ask:** "If we just did the missed-call piece for $179/mo — would that be in budget? Most shops upgrade to the full bundle within 60 days once they see the data."

[Down-sell to $179 only when budget is the real blocker. Often they'll find $497 once u show ROI math.]

---

## 3. "I'll think about it / send me info"

**Acknowledge:** "Yep, I'd want to think about it too."
**Reframe:** "Real talk — 'send me info' usually means 'no but I don't wanna say no.' Which is fine. Two questions:"
**Ask:** "(1) Is there anything specific I could clarify in 60 sec right now? (2) If the answer's just no, would u tell me what'd have to be true for u to say yes — so I'm not wasting ur time later?"

[This converts 30% of "send me info" into either a real conversation or a clean no. Both > limbo.]

---

## 4. "My wife / my admin handles the phones"

**Acknowledge:** "Smart — having a real person beats voicemail every time."
**Reframe:** "Question — does she love it? Most spouses I talk to want to be doing something else. AI handles the inbound, she handles the stuff AI can't do — payroll, scheduling techs, vendor calls. It's a promotion for her, not a replacement."
**Ask:** "Worth showing her the demo together? I do those Tue/Wed afternoons."

[CAREFUL: if his spouse IS the actual brain of the business, this can backfire. Read the room. If he says "she likes it that way" — back off, soft circle: "Got it. Maybe in 6mo if call volume gets too crazy for her, I'll check back."]

---

## 5. "We just signed up for [Rosie / Goodcall / answering service]"

**Acknowledge:** "Got it — what made u pick them?"
**Reframe:** "How long u been using them? [Listen.] When the contract's up — or u're hitting a wall on what they can do — call me. Most shops outgrow basic receptionist after 4-6 months when the quote follow-up + collections piece becomes obvious."
**Ask:** "Want me to send u a side-by-side comparison so when u re-evaluate u're not starting from zero?"

[Don't fight an active competitor. Plant the seed for renewal. Track these in a "follow up Q4" list.]

---

## 6. "I don't trust giving AI my customer list / data"

**Acknowledge:** "Reasonable. Data lives where?"
**Reframe:** "Couple things — data's in a private Supabase instance, encrypted at rest, only u can see it. We never train on ur customer data. And here's the actual answer: u can run it for 30 days w fake/test data first. If u don't trust it after that, u get refund + we delete everything we touched."
**Ask:** "Would a 30-day test on a side number — not ur main shop number — work?"

[The test on a side number is the killer offer. Removes risk.]

---

## 7. "I don't have time to learn another tool"

**Acknowledge:** "Hear u — software fatigue is real."
**Reframe:** "Setup is literally: u tell us ur services + price ranges + ur calendar, we forward ur phone, done. Onboarding is 20 minutes, then it just runs. There's nothing to log into daily."
**Ask:** "Could u find 20 min Wednesday afternoon? I'll do the setup with u live, u walk away with it running."

---

## 8. "Sounds too good to be true / what's the catch"

**Acknowledge:** "I'd be skeptical too."
**Reframe:** "Honest catches: (1) AI isn't perfect — it'll mess up maybe 5% of calls, especially heavy accents or super complex questions. (2) The first 2 weeks it's learning ur business — u'll get a few weird bookings. (3) U gotta actually use the dashboard to check on it the first month or u won't catch the edge cases."
**Ask:** "If those catches are deal-breakers, totally fine. If u can live with them in exchange for catching 30+ jobs/month u'd otherwise miss — let's do the demo."

[Honesty disarms skeptics. Listing the actual catches builds more trust than denying them.]

---

## RED FLAG OBJECTIONS — Disqualify Fast

| Objection | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| "Send me a contract first" | Lawyering up before value seen — over-thinker | "We're MTM no contract. Demo first, contract later." If they push, walk. |
| "How are u different from Rosie/Goodcall" multiple times | Price-shopping | They'll never pay $497. Down-sell to $179 or move on. |
| "Can u integrate with [obscure CRM]" | Integration hunter, not buyer | "We integrate w Jobber/HCP/ST. Custom integrations come post-customer-100." |
| "Do u have case studies / references" before demo | Buying-by-committee mindset | Send 1 case study (when u have one). If they ask for 5 — they're not buying soon. |

---

## THE GOLDEN RULE OF OBJECTIONS

**Most objections aren't objections — they're requests for more information.**

Before u counter, repeat their objection back to them in different words. Half the time they'll answer it themselves:

> "When u say it's too much — too much compared to what?"
> "When u say u'll think about it — what specifically r u thinking about?"
> "When u say AI won't work — have u tried one recently or is this from years back?"

The asking is the close.
