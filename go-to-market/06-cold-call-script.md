# COLD CALL SCRIPT — HVAC Owner Direct

**Goal of call:** book a 30-min demo. Nothing else.
**Length target:** 90 seconds to "yes I'll take a demo" or "no thanks". No 10-min calls.
**Mindset:** ur not selling, ur disqualifying. Most won't be a fit. Find the ones who are, fast.

---

## THE OPENER (memorize verbatim)

> "Hey {{first_name}}, this is Peter from BellAveGo — I'm gonna be honest, this is a cold call. I've got 30 seconds to tell u why I called, and then u can hang up if it's not relevant. Fair?"

**Why it works:** Pattern interrupt. "Cold call" admission disarms. "30 seconds" earns permission. "Fair" gets a yes.

**Expected response:** ~70% say "ok go ahead." ~20% hang up immediately. ~10% "make it quick."

If they hang up — fine. Move to next. Don't take it personally. We're a numbers game until customer 5.

---

## THE HOOK (15 seconds — say this exactly)

> "I help HVAC shops in {{city}} stop losing jobs to missed calls. I looked at {{business_name}} on Google — u've got {{review_count}} reviews and a clean shop. Quick math says ur missing 30–60 calls/month worth maybe $XX,XXX in booked jobs. Either I'm wrong about the numbers — or u've already solved this. Which is it?"

**Three possible responses:**

### A) "U're wrong about the numbers"
> "Cool — what's ur actual setup for after-hours calls?"
[Listen. Take notes. Don't pitch. They'll tell u the real story in 30 seconds.]

### B) "We've already solved that"
> "Awesome — what r u using? [Listen.] How's it working for u?"
[If they say "voicemail"/"my wife"/"my admin" — those aren't solved. Press gently.]
[If they say "Rosie" or "answering service" — qualify pain. "What do u wish it did better?"]

### C) "Tell me more"
> "Cool. The short version: AI receptionist + quote follow-up + collections + review replies, $497/mo. Replaces about 70% of an office manager's job. First month free. Want a 30-min demo Wednesday or Thursday?"

---

## THE QUALIFY (only if they're engaging)

3 questions. Quick.

> "1. How many calls a week do u think hit voicemail?"
> "2. Who follows up on quotes — u, or do they kinda just sit there?"
> "3. If I told u we'd add 4–6 booked jobs a month for $497, would u test it for 30 days w no risk?"

**If they say YES to #3:** book demo NOW. Don't email later. Open Cal.com on screen, lock the slot.
**If they say "let me think":** that's a no. "Got it — should I check back in 30 days or just leave u alone?"
**If they say "send me info":** that's also a no, but softer. "I will. What email's best? Anything specific u wanna see in there?"

---

## THE CLOSE

> "Cool — Wednesday at 2pm or Thursday at 10am ur time, which works?"

**Hard rule:** never offer 3+ slots. Two options = decision. Three options = procrastination.

After they pick:
> "Great. I'll send a calendar invite to {{email}} — u'll get a link to the demo. It's 30 min, I'll show u exactly how it works for {{business_name}} specifically. Anything u want me to dig into beforehand?"

[Pause. Let them ask. They'll tell u what matters.]

> "Talk Wednesday."

Hang up.

---

## VOICEMAIL SCRIPT (when they don't pick up — 80% of dials)

> "Hey {{first_name}}, Peter from BellAveGo. I'll be brief — I help HVAC shops in {{city}} stop losing jobs to missed calls. Did some quick math on {{business_name}} — looks like u might be missing ~$XX,XXX/month. Worth a 10-min look. Call me back at 773-710-9565 or text 'demo' to that same number. Either way, take care."

**Why text option:** half of contractors won't call back but will text. Lower friction.

---

## RHYTHM — DAILY

- **20 cold calls/day, 5 days/week.**
- Block time 1–3pm local: that's lunch break for owner-operators, they pick up phones then.
- Track: dials / connects / qualifies / demo booked.
- Connect rate: target >25%. Demo book rate from connect: target >15%. = ~3 demos booked/wk just from cold calls.

---

## TONE NOTES

- Speak like a contractor, not a SaaS bro. No "synergy", no "ROI uplift", no "leverage."
- Use "shop" not "business." "Truck" not "vehicle." "Booked job" not "conversion."
- Curse occasionally if they do (mirror, don't lead).
- Move fast. Contractors have no patience for slow talkers.
- Smile while u talk — it carries through the phone.
- Don't apologize for calling. Don't say "is this a good time?" — answer is always no.

---

## WHEN TO HANG UP THE CALL

Hang up YOURSELF (politely) if:
- They're 2+ years from buying ("we just hired an admin last week")
- They run a franchise (decisions made elsewhere)
- They're hostile ("not interested" 3x — respect it, move on)
- The conversation isn't moving toward demo or disqualify in 3 minutes

> "Sounds like the timing isn't there. I'll get out of ur hair. Take care."

Move to next dial. Speed matters more than any single call.
